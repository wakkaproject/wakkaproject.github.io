About WAKKA

WAKKA is a digital NFT project inspired by five rescued cats: Mee, Kuro, Kikki, Miku, and Wakka.
“WAKKA” means “circle” in Japanese — representing connection, love, and care.
This project preserves their unique stories and shares their charm with the world.

Meet Our Cats

Mee

A calm, sweet girl with rare hachiware markings. She loves humans but prefers to be alone around cats.

Kuro

A black cat with sleepy eyes. He's a lone wolf, gentle but sometimes grumpy. He's very attached to people.

Kikki

Fluffy and kind, Kikki is loved by everyone. He’s athletic, sweet, and sleeps next to both people and cats.

Miku

A playful, round-eyed black girl. She loves cats more than humans and always brings energy to the room.

Wakka

A large white cat with a human-like attitude. Calm, funny, and a peacemaker when fights happen. The heart of the family.

FAQ / Roadmap

Q: What inspired you to start the WAKKA project?

A: I adopted rescued cats and they became my beloved family. They each have unique personalities and brought joy and meaning to my life. I wanted to preserve their presence digitally and share their charm with the world as a circle of happiness.

Q: Why did you choose NFTs?

A: Because NFTs last forever, and it’s the best way to let people around the world meet my cats.

Q: How do you care for their health?

A: WAKKA and KURO are currently on treatment for urinary stones. I visit the vet immediately if any cat is unwell. NFT sales are used to support their health and happiness.

Q: What's your future goal?

A: I want more people to meet my cats and smile. If WAKKA becomes known globally, I’d love to create a WAKKA meme coin.

Q: Any message to visitors?

A: My cats are full of charm and fun. I hope you enjoy following their adventures!
